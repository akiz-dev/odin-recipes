Chicken Curry with Coconut

Description

The classic cheesesteak recipe features thinly sliced beef, sautéed onions, and melted cheese all piled high on a soft hoagie roll. It´s a hearty, flavorful sandwich that brings the taste of Philadelfia right to your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450 g) ribeye steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil or butter
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 4 slices provolone cheese (or Cheez Whiz / American cheese, if preferred)
  • 2 hoagie rolls of long sandwich rools
  • Optional toppings: sautéed bell peppers, mushrooms, or hot peppers

Steps

  1. Prepare the beef:
    Place the ribeye steak in the freezer for about 30 minutes to firm it up slightly. Then, slice it as thinly as possible across the again.

  2. Cook the onions:
    Heat the oil or butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add slice onions and cook for 5-7 minutes, until soft and lightly caramelized. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  3. Cook the beef:
    In the same skillet, add the sliced beef. Season with salt and pepper. Cook for 3-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until browned and cooked through.

  4. Combine onions and beef:
    Return the onions to the skillet and mix them with the beef. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.

  5. Melt the cheese:
    Lay the cheese slices over beef and onion mixture. COver the pan for about 1 minute, just until the cheese melts.

  6. Assemble the sandwich:
    Slice the hoagie rolls open and slightly toast them if desired. Spoon the cheesy beef and onion mixture evenly into each roll

  7. Serve and enjoy:
    Serve warm with your favorites sides, like fries or chips.
